Query Exploration Notebook

This document offers a structure you might use for keeping track of your preliminary results with the various query options as you explore your models. You can copy-paste relevant results into each section below, along with any initial notes on what seems significant or worth further exploration. If you are working with multiple models, make sure to keep track of which model you're using for any results you include!

Clusters

In this section, record any clusters that are of interest, as well as your notes on what you think they might be showing or notes on any terms the clusters suggest you might want to query.

“Closest to” Queries

Use this space to record the results of queries for single terms. Make sure to include the term! What were the most significant neighbors (with the highest cosine similarity) and what was the cosine similarity?

Addition Queries

Use this section to record the results for queries that use the addition function, which looks at the combined vectors for multiple terms (term 1 + term 2).

Subtraction Queries

Use this space to record the results for queries that use the subtraction operation, which looks at the space between two terms—that is, the terms that are closest to one term when the contexts for the second term are subtracted (term 1 - term 2).

Analogies

Use this section to record the results of queries for the analogy function, which subtracts the contexts associated with one word from another then adds a third term (term 1 - term 2 + term A). This will generate results that associate with the difference between the first two terms plus the contexts of the third term.
